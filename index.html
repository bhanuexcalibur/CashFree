Part 01 Messaging Audit

cashfree.com/cross-border  ·  Where the current story leaves gaps

Three surfaces where the exports messaging could work harder for the exporter actually reading it

✕ What's there now

The cross-border page leads with product categories: "Global Collections," "International Payment Gateway," and feature bullets around currencies and settlement timelines. It reads like a spec sheet. A SaaS founder landing here after Googling "how to get paid from US clients India" has to do a lot of work to understand which product is even relevant to them, and whether Cashfree is significantly different from what they're already using.

✓ Where it could go

Lead with the situation the exporter is already in. Something like: "Your US client just asked for an invoice. You're about to send your PayPal link. There's a better way." That single line does more to qualify the reader and create urgency than four feature bullets about settlement rails. The product detail follows once the exporter feels seen.

The core issue: The current page assumes the reader already knows they need a cross-border payments product and is evaluating features. Most of Cashfree's target exporters are still in the "I don't know there's something better" stage. Starting with their situation rather than the product's capabilities changes the entire conversion dynamic.
✕ Current framing

"Collect payments in 15+ foreign currencies. Auto-generate e-FIRA within 24 hours. T+1 to T+2 settlement." These are accurate, useful facts. But they're aimed at someone who already speaks the language: a finance executive or compliance officer, not the SaaS founder who doesn't know what FIRA stands for and isn't sure if they even need one.

✓ Rewrite for the actual user

"Every payment you receive from an international client automatically comes with the compliance document your CA needs at tax time. You don't have to track it down, request it, or explain to anyone what it is." Same feature, completely different register. The exporter reading this understands why it matters without knowing what an e-FIRA is.

The translation problem: Cashfree's cross-border product has genuinely useful compliance automation built in. The gap is that the copy assumes familiarity with the compliance requirements. Translating those features into the actual day-to-day relief they provide unlocks a much larger addressable audience, specifically the mid-market businesses and D2C founders who haven't had a finance team educate them on what they even need.
✕ The problem Cashfree doesn't yet name

Competitors like Skydo and Xflow have built their entire brand around "purpose-built for exporters." Their messaging is tight: zero FX markup, instant FIRA, built only for international payments. Cashfree's cross-border positioning is harder to land because it's part of a broader product, and the current messaging doesn't explain why that's actually an advantage for the exporter.

✓ The real differentiator worth owning

Cashfree is the only player that handles both the international receipt and everything that happens inside India: payroll, vendor payments, tax disbursals, all in one place. "Collect globally. Run everything else through the same account." That's a genuine advantage for a growing business that no export-only player can match. It's just not being said yet.

The positioning opportunity: Rather than competing with Skydo on "who's more purpose-built for exports," Cashfree wins by reframing the category. Export-only tools solve one problem and create a new one: you now have a separate platform for international collections that doesn't talk to your domestic stack. Cashfree is the only option where global and domestic operations live in the same place. That's the story worth telling, and it's not being told right now.
Part 02 GTM Brief

Export Segment GTM  ·  90-Day Launch Brief

How I'd build the motion for Indian exporters: SaaS, D2C, freelancers, mid-market

Q3 2026 Draft

The Opportunity

India's service exports crossed $330B last year. The businesses doing that exporting are mostly using PayPal, wire transfers through their bank, or export-only niche tools like Skydo. None of them know that Cashfree now gives them virtual accounts in USD, EUR, GBP, CAD, and AUD, settling in INR at live FX rates, with auto-generated FIRA per payment.

The PA-CB license, the $53M raise, and the 140+ currency upgrade from May 2025 are all proof points that Cashfree is serious about this segment. The product is ready. The market doesn't know that yet.

Why Now

Three things have shifted in the last 12 months that make this the right window. The RBI PA-CB framework has matured: more businesses now understand they need a licensed aggregator and not just any payment link. Competitors have grown the awareness of the category without owning it. And Cashfree's recent 140+ currency announcement gives us a tangible, first-of-its-kind claim to lead with.

We're not creating a new category. We're winning a category that's already forming around us.

Core Message

You're already doing global business. Your payment setup shouldn't make you feel like you're not. Cashfree gives Indian exporters virtual accounts in the currencies their clients use, so you get paid the way your client is comfortable paying, without the wire transfer fees, the PayPal percentage, or the three-day wait to know if the money actually arrived.

Everything that happens after the money lands, from vendor payouts to salaries to tax payments, runs in the same account. That's what makes Cashfree different from every export-only alternative.

Channel Mix

LinkedIn: Founders and finance leads at Indian SaaS and D2C companies. Content focused on the real cost of PayPal, the FIRA pain point, and the "both domestic and global in one place" angle.

Communities: SaaSBoomi, D2C Insider, IndiaHacks, IndiaMART. These are where the actual target customer is having conversations about scaling internationally.

Partnerships: CAs and GST consultants who actively field questions about international payment compliance. They're already trusted advisors to the exporter and have every incentive to recommend a cleaner collection process.

30-60-90 Execution

Days 1-30 Foundational work. Audit all existing cross-border pages and sales materials. Interview 5-8 current cross-border customers to understand how they found us, what they expected, and where the first confusion point is. Build the messaging framework with three buyer registers: SaaS founder, D2C brand, freelancer/consultant. Align sales on the new "global plus domestic" positioning before it goes live anywhere external.
Days 31-60 Launch assets. Rewrite the cross-border landing page based on new positioning. Build a comparison one-pager (Cashfree vs. export-only alternatives) for sales. Launch the first LinkedIn content series with a mix of educational content on e-FIRA, SWIFT costs, and FX markup, written from the exporter's perspective. Start community seeding in SaaSBoomi and D2C Insider forums.
Days 61-90 Amplify and measure. Run a paid campaign targeting founders and finance leads at companies with international revenue signals (job postings mentioning overseas clients, LinkedIn about sections with global markets). Host one webinar with a CA partner on "how Indian exporters should actually structure their international collections for tax time." Track pipeline contribution, not just top-of-funnel metrics.

Part 04 Copy Samples

Four pieces of actual copy across different channels, written for the export buyer. These aren't templates. Each one is written for a specific moment in the journey where the exporter is making a real decision, and each one is trying to earn the next step rather than just describe the product.

Landing page — Global Collections hero Web
Your US client pays you like a local vendor. You get paid in INR by tomorrow.
When you share your Cashfree Global Collections account details, your client in New York pays via ACH. Your client in London pays via Faster Payments. They're paying into a local account, in a currency they know, through a method they already use. The money converts to INR at live exchange rates and lands in your Indian bank account. Every payment comes with an e-FIRA, automatically, so your CA doesn't need to ask for it at the end of the quarter. No wire transfer. No 3-5% PayPal cut. No chasing confirmation emails for three days.
Why it works: Starts from the client's experience, not the product feature, because the exporter cares most about whether their client finds it easy. Explains the mechanism in plain language. Ends by naming the three actual pain points in reverse order of how they usually get discovered: cost last, friction first. The e-FIRA line treats the reader like someone who knows what this is without explaining it.
LinkedIn — Organic content post Social
The hidden cost of using PayPal for international invoices (that most founders don't calculate until year 3)
Say you're billing $8,000/month in international revenue through PayPal. The standard 4.4% international transaction fee means you're leaving roughly $352/month on the table. That's around Rs 29,000 every month. Rs 3.5 lakh a year. That's one good senior hire, or one quarter of runway, depending on where you are. Now add the fact that PayPal settlements can take 3-5 business days, you don't get a per-transaction FIRA automatically, and your US client has to fund their PayPal account instead of just paying from their bank. At a certain revenue point, the math just stops making sense. Worth knowing what your actual options are before you scale another vertical that runs on the same stack.
Why it works: The hook works because "year 3" implies the reader has been missing this for a while, which is more uncomfortable than just saying "you're paying too much." The math is specific enough to feel real without being abstract, and it's tied to something the reader can visualise (a hire, runway). Doesn't pitch Cashfree explicitly. Ends by opening the door for the reader to explore without demanding it.
Sales enablement — Battle card opening (vs. export-only tools) Sales
When the prospect says: "We're already using Skydo / Xflow for international payments."
Don't argue with the choice. Validate it, then ask one question: "That makes sense. Those tools are built specifically for export collections and they do that well. The question we usually hear once a business scales past a certain point is: what do you use for everything that happens after the international payment lands? Your vendor disbursals, payroll, domestic payouts, all of that. Are those in a separate system right now?" If the answer is yes, the conversation moves naturally. Export-only tools are great at one thing and require a separate stack for everything else. Cashfree is the only account where global collections and domestic operations live in the same place.
Why it works: The instinct when a prospect names a competitor is to list your advantages. That triggers defensiveness. This card tells the rep to agree with the choice first, which earns trust, then opens a different gap entirely. The question is about their domestic stack, not about the competitor's weaknesses. It reframes the conversation from "which international tool is better" to "is one tool actually enough," which is a question Cashfree uniquely wins.

Why it works: Doesn't lead with "thanks for attending" or pitch the product. Opens with a behaviour that positions the reader as someone who's already thinking about this problem. The e-FIRA focus is specific enough to be useful to someone who attended, so it rewards attention. The CTA is low-commitment and framed as a service, not a sales call.
